## Chennai Neighborhoods & Food Culture

### T. Nagar (Pondy Bazaar Area)
**Famous For**: Shopping district with quick bites and late-night food
**Peak Times**: 6 PM - 10 PM (Evening/Night)
**Vibe**: Fast-paced, crowded, diverse food options
**Demographics**: Shoppers, mixed crowd, all income levels

**Top Street Foods**:

1. **Kothu Parotta** (Buhari, Burma Bazaar junction)
   - Best time: After 8 PM
   - Price: ₹80-150
   - Type: Non-veg (chicken/mutton/egg), Veg option available
   - Description: Crispy shredded parotta mixed with spicy gravy
   - Local tip: Say "Romba kaara venum" for extra spicy
   - Mood: Spicy & Bold, Night Snack
   
2. **Atho** (Burma Influence, Burma Bazaar street stalls)
   - Best time: 7 PM - 11 PM
   - Price: ₹60-100
   - Type: Veg/Non-veg both
   - Description: Yellow noodles with tangy sauce and spices
   - Mood: Night Snack
   
3. **Filter Coffee & Bajji** (Saravana Bhavan, Ratna Cafe)
   - Best time: 4-7 PM (Evening tea time)
   - Price: ₹30-60
   - Type: Pure Veg
   - Description: Classic Chennai evening combo
   - Mood: Light & Healthy, Evening Tea
   
4. **Pani Puri & Bhel** (Ranganathan Street stalls)
   - Best time: 5-9 PM
   - Price: ₹30-50
   - Type: Pure Veg
   - Description: Tangy, spicy street food favorite
   - Mood: Pure Veg, Evening
   
5. **Idli Sambar** (Ratna Cafe)
   - Best time: 7-10 AM
   - Price: ₹40-70
   - Type: Pure Veg
   - Description: Soft idlis with aromatic sambar
   - Mood: Breakfast/Morning
   
6. **Masala Vada & Tea** (Local tea kadais)
   - Best time: 4-7 PM
   - Price: ₹20-40
   - Type: Pure Veg
   - Description: Crispy lentil fritters with masala tea
   - Mood: Evening Tea

## Time-Based Eating Habits

### Morning (6 AM - 10 AM)
**Culture**: Breakfast is sacred. Hot idli, dosa, pongal with filter coffee.
**Best areas**: Mylapore (traditional), Anna Nagar (quality), T. Nagar (quick)
**Peak foods**: Idli, dosa, vada, filter coffee, keerai vadai

### Afternoon (12 PM - 3 PM)
**Culture**: Usually meals at home. Street food less common.
**Exception**: Office areas (Mount Road, Velachery) have lunch options
**Peak foods**: Thalis, meals, biriyani

### Evening (4 PM - 7 PM)
**Culture**: PEAK street food time! Tea + snacks is a ritual.
**Best areas**: Marina Beach (sundal!), Anna Nagar, T. Nagar
**Peak foods**: Bajji, sundal, bonda, filter coffee, murukku

### Night (8 PM - 12 AM)
**Culture**: Heavy foods. Kothu Parotta peak time.
**Best areas**: Mount Road (Buhari - THE spot), Velachery, T. Nagar
**Peak foods**: Kothu parotta, shawarma, atho, parotta with salna

### Late Night (After 12 AM)
**Culture**: Limited options. Only 24/7 spots active.
**Best**: Mount Road area, some Velachery spots
**Peak foods**: Kothu parotta, tea

## Local Tamil Terms & Usage

### Addressing People
- **Anna** - Elder brother (respectful for men, ANY age vendor/shopkeeper)
  - Usage: "Anna, oru plate kothu parotta"
- **Akka** - Elder sister (respectful for women)
  - Usage: "Akka, sundal irukka?"
- **Mama** - Uncle (casual for middle-aged men)
- **Thambi** - Younger brother

### Food & Shop Terms
- **Kadai** - Shop
  - "Tea kadai" = Tea shop
  - "Jannal kadai" = Window shop (Mylapore specialty)
- **Mess** - Small restaurant (NOT messy! Traditional eatery)
- **Thattu Kadai** - Small plate shop (cheap eats)
- **Annan** - Boiled eggs (from street vendors)

### Taste & Preferences
- **Kaara** - Spicy
  - "Kaara bajji" = Spicy bajji
- **Romba kaara venum** - Want it very spicy
- **Kaara vendam** - Don't want spicy
- **Spicy vendam** - Don't want spicy (English-Tamil mix)
- **Pulippu** - Tangy/sour
  - "Pulippu jasthi" = Extra tangy
- **Salt aathadi** - More salt (when ordering)

### Ordering Phrases
- **"Evlo?"** - How much? (price)
- **"Parcel"** - Takeaway
- **"Half plate"** - Smaller portion
- **"Full saapadu"** - Full meal
- **"Oru plate"** - One plate
- **"Rendhu plate"** - Two plates

### Common Expressions
- **"Seri"** - Okay/Alright
- **"Nalla irukku"** - It's good (praising food)
- **"Super"** - Excellent (commonly used)

## Cultural Notes & Etiquette

### Veg vs Non-Veg Culture
- **Mylapore**: Predominantly vegetarian (Brahmin area), respect this
- **T. Nagar**: Mixed, everything available
- **Marina/Beaches**: More veg-friendly (family areas)
- **Mount Road**: Non-veg haven at night
- **Anna Nagar**: Mostly veg, family-oriented

### Eating Etiquette
- Most street food eaten standing or squatting (normal!)
- Hand-eating is common (especially kothu parotta)
- Sharing food is cultural norm
- Banana leaf plates common for traditional items
- Wash hands before/after eating at street stalls

### Timing is CRUCIAL
- **Marina Beach Sundal**: ONLY 5-8 PM, not other times!
- **Breakfast items**: Best 7-10 AM, not available afternoon
- **Kothu Parotta**: Peak taste after 9 PM
- **Filter Coffee**: Morning and evening (4-7 PM) are best times

### Payment & Tipping
- Most street food: Pay immediately after receiving
- Prices usually fixed, minimal bargaining
- Tipping not expected at street stalls
- Carry small change (₹10, ₹20, ₹50 notes)

### Hygiene Tips
- Crowded stalls = high turnover = fresh food
- Avoid exposed food in afternoon sun
- Evening stalls generally fresher
- Popular iconic places (Buhari, Murugan) are safer
- Trust your instincts!

## Seasonal Variations

### Summer (March-June)
**Preferences**: Cold drinks, light snacks, juices, ice cream
**Avoid**: Very heavy, oily foods
**Best**: Fresh juices, coconut water, light dosas

### Monsoon (October-December)
**Peak Season**: BAJJI/PAKODA TIME!
**Preferences**: Hot, fried snacks with tea
**Best**: Bajji, bonda, hot masala tea, pakodas
**Note**: Rainy days = automatic bajji cravings

### Winter (December-February)
**Best Season**: All foods flourish, comfortable weather
**Preferences**: Everything is enjoyable
**Special**: Filter coffee tastes better in cool weather
